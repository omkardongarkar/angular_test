The top factors that drive returns based on the analyzed records can be categorized as:

  1. Product Quality Issues:
    1. A significant number (48%) of customers returned items due to product defects or overall poor quality. Examples range from non-operational electronic items to quality complaints about clothing.
    2. There was spike in laptops not connecting to the networks. Likely most common factor the patch update on 12/13/2024.
    3. There were instances of the product malfunctioning (20%), such as appliances making noises, hairdryers not working, and screens flickering.
    4. Some customers (4%) received damaged items like chipped knives, broken coffee makers, or cracked screens.
    5. In about 24% of conversations, discrepancies in product quality, such as defective items, wrong size, or non-functioning goods led to return intentions.
  2. Incorrect Product or Misfit:
    1. Clothing items with incorrect fit or size lead to a fraction of returns (12%).
    2. Customers (10%) returned items such as clothing or shoes due to size issues.
  3. Return Process and Policy Dissatisfaction:
    1. A noticeable percentage of customers (36%) complained about the return process and policy. This includes slow refund processes (delayed for 3-5 business days) and perceived limitations within the return policy.
    2. Frustration with policy limitations and exceptions, particularly around the return of worn or used items and customized gifts, accounted for another 20% of complaints.
    3. There were instances of customers dissatisfied with account services, with main complaints related to unprocessed refunds, unexpected charges, and poor customer service.
  4. System and Service Issues:
    1. Long resolution times for problems, payment processing, and account access issues resulted in customers (36%) wanting to cancel subscriptions or services.
    2. Technical glitches such as failures during checkout, inability to update payment details, or password reset complications led to heightened user dissatisfaction.
    3. Billing errors such as double charges or erroneous payments accounted for 12% of complaints, with dissatisfaction with customer support a factor in an additional 8%.
  5. Order and Delivery Errors:
    1. Incorrect items delivered or missing from the order (12%) also led to returns.

In conclusion, focusing on improving product quality, refining return policies, speeding up the refund process, addressing system glitches, and enhancing customer service could help significantly reduce these return-related issues.
